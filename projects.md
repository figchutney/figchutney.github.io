Below is a (short, but growing!) list of projects I've worked on for fun.

## 🥘 [Fagiolo](https://github.com/figchutney/fagiolo)

Me and my girlfiend get bored trying to remember what meals we like and which ingredients go into them when we do a food shop. Fagiolo gives you a user-defined number of random meals along with their ingredients. If you get one you don't fancy / don't like, you can easily swap it out for another. The name? It's 'bean' in Italian.

It's hosted on Heroku - [have a play around](https://fagiolo.herokuapp.com/) (give it 30 seconds or so to start up - I'm on Heroku's free tier so the dyno goes to sleep after innactivity 🙃)

## ⛰ [Wainlog](https://github.com/figchutney/wainlog)

Some people (like me) enjoy ticking off which [Wainwrights](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Wainwrights) they've climbed in the Lake District. Doing it in a Google Sheet is a bit boring, and other websites I've found are quite bloated / not very pretty. Wainlog is a simple, clean place to keep track of what you've climbed.

This isn't *quite* finished yet, but there's a little demo vid [in the README](https://github.com/figchutney/wainlog).

## 💻 [Advent of Code Solutions](https://github.com/figchutney/advent-of-code)

My solutions to the [Advent of Code](https://adventofcode.com/) puzzles. I stick my 'prod-style' solutions in `puzzles/<YEAR>/<DAY>/main.py`, with accompanying tests in `tests/<YEAR>/<DAY>`. If I think it's interesting/worth it, I'll put a stripped back, simple solution in `puzzles/<YEAR>/<DAY>/simple.py`
